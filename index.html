Weekly Retrospectives

Week 1

04/05/26 - 10/05/26

What went well this sprint?

I was able to locate a problem that is relatively niche and not overly used. The topic has the potential to diversify my design portfolio. As other topics explored seemed to favour minimalist design, which I have done plenty of prior. I was also able to identify and ask potential target users to interview. These are also people who are more easily accessible for follow ups and observations. I’ve accomplished my main goal of a Problem statement and research planning. There are some minor elements that will be caught up on, but I’m happy the main goals are reached.

What did not go well this sprint?

The area I have chosen was not my original choice. It was part of what I brainstormed. I think it took a little longer than I expected to lock in a problem and area. I struggled narrowing it down from my options. I think part of this is because I kept thinking forward and looking at why this might be difficult to do. E.g. lack of access to people I can interview, or the potential scope. I also struggled with information, there are not a lot of research available in this specific topic of ‘book slump’. Even with the journal articles, plenty of research are not aligned with what I’m looking for. E.g. research on readers with most articles looking at children or secondary school students. These will most likely not be part of target audience.

What did you learn?

I think being broader in choosing fields would be beneficial. I think I narrowed myself down by thinking forward on potentially why certain topics or problems may be difficult to tackle. It is probably still commendable to think of these issues initially. But it probably has to be more surface level as not to erase the option completely. Thinking about it now, once you start researching, or conducting user interviews, you might find a way to pivot and narrow down the problem further in a way this is more meaningful and manageable. This is the first sprint, so there currently is nothing to review, change or try currently.

What will you do next sprint, with this lesson in mind?

Again, being the first sprint there are not a lot of previous measures I can action. However, I will continue on what I mentioned previously about keeping it broad to not box myself in a certain direction. I will do this once I go further in to my secondary research as well as conducting user interviews or observations. I will have to ensure that my questions remain open and not lead my interviewees in a biased direction. I don’t believe I’m the most knowledgeable in this topic, and I will utilise this to keep my options open. This may be altering my problem statement or focusing on a specific aspect of it.

Week 2

11/05/26 - 17/05/26

What went well this sprint?

I was able to begin my primary research. It felt like a gold mine of information, especially in pin pointing sort of existing trends that I found in my secondary research.Another goldmine I found was a 2025 New Zealand based study on reading habits. This meant that I didn’t have to do things like surveys for broader information on my topic. I think I mostly met my sprint goals, however, I underestimated how long it would take me to complete my interviews, this gave way to delays in completing other tasks.

What did not go well this sprint?

I felt a little worried initially because there was such a lack of secondary research available. And those that were there, were things like personal blogs, magazine or newspaper articles. Though I would say, the testimonials in those articles did prove useful in the end. Building on what I mentioned in what went well, because of the lack of secondary research, I really needed to capture more information in my interviews. This is the first time, I felt that my interviews were quite long and arduous. Especially organising them and extracting trends and key insights. I ended up taking much longer than I originally expected.

What did you learn?

I thought that because I had so much to get through with interviews, doing a focus group of 2 interviewees would save me time. I did this, and the interview took a very long time (45 min). The problem I encountered was it was hard to focus on going back and forth with interviewees - In a sense that I’m trying to take in what one person has said to really understand and have great follow up questions. As a little side, I think I tried to capture both fringe information and specific information as to not box myself in. This is what I set out to do in my previous sprint. I think I did this well, I just didn’t think it would make interviews so long.

What will you do next sprint, with this lesson in mind?

I still have potentially 1 to 2 interviews left, although I probably have enough if I left it. I currently have 5 individuals interviewed. 3 are within my target audience and 2 are not. I feel like I need to really see what works best for me. Definitely will only be doing single interviews. But I’m still on the fence on whether I work better and have better output in typing out the answers in the interview or recording then transcribing. If I get into a similar interview scenario, I will attempt to condense my questions and really stick to the barebone materials that are essential. But I feel that this is very situational. I might very well need all of the information from those questions.

Week 3

18/05/26 - 24/05/26

What went well this sprint?

From my interviews, I was able to get some understanding on how my target audience used ‘Goodreads’. This helped significantly as this is the single largest competitor, albeit slightly indirect to my solution. I was now also able to consolidate my research to give me a direction in what my solution may look like. It gave me good insight, as for example, my initial solution was being able to converse or utilise AI in having a conversation with the user. But I found that though not all, a significant portion does not like having these types of interactions with AI.

What did not go well this sprint?

I got stuck. I found out about Storygraph. It wasn’t originally in my radar because I didn’t know about it and none of my interviewees mentioned it at all. I found that it has a more comprehensive recommendation process than Goodreads, and I thought that it pretty much overlapped with my solution. I was researching different ways to pivot but I felt like it kept circling back to a great recommendation function. This was quite frustrating and put quite a hold in my progress. Due to time constraints, I was also not able to conduct my last 2 interviews.

What did you learn?

I was not able to conduct my last 2 interviews, but due to the detailed questions in my interviews, I still had actionable data in progressing with my solution. I think for next time, I will need to scope out potential competitors independently. I was overly confident and reliant on the information I had from my interviewees. In that, Goodreads was the only option anyone and everyone utilised. But this was also because there was such a lack of secondary research available. I was able to learn to individual pick out differences in my potential solution against such a close competitor as Storygraph. Though a slight pivot, not an astronomical change.

What will you do next sprint, with this lesson in mind?

For my next project, I would need to be more thorough about competitor research. Maybe even a quick scope prior to looking too closely in to a solution. I think because the previous Summatives didn’t have a mandatory Competitor Analysis requirement, it was not heavily thought of. But from what I’ve learned, this also contains a wealth of information that I will be incorporating in future projects. It was slow for me during this Summative, but practicing pivoting and refining aspect such as Problem Statements and Solutions would be more common. I initially assumed there would be little change to these from the beginning. Now that I have been disillusioned, this feels like a healthy and creative practice to employ.

Week 4

25/05/26 - 31/05/26

What went well this sprint?

I had a moment of clarity in trying to decide what key user flows I wanted to do and more on what I wanted to include in them. It really helped when I was bouncing Ideas of another individual but what really helped the most was explaining these ideas and taking them step-by-step. The pitch feedback was very helpful. I think also them coming from 3rd year students gave me some insight on how they specifically thought and viewed things. I think the sprint goal was only partially met. I haven’t managed a full lo fi with testing.

What did not go well this sprint?

The pitch was a new experience, and traversing the requirements were not easy. First designing the slides were a little more challenging because I’m not at the stage where a colour palette and branding is finalised. Second, this is the first time we were really ‘pitching’. Previous summatives had presentations but still felt different to pitches. This lead me to be more data and information lead rather than the overarching goal of convincing stakeholders of an idea.

What did you learn?

In terms of pitch, I think keeping to an overall idea is great. But I think shifting perspectives, It may work better if I started with a final goal in mind of what I wanted to drive home with my pitch. Then it possibly might be easier to work backwards and decide on what elements are necessary to keep an overarching theme and reach the goal. This may also help the struggle I had with deciding what to put in my pitch and what to leave out. My solution has lots of different elements some big, some small but they’re needed to make sure the solution actually works.

What will you do next sprint, with this lesson in mind?

With this in mind, for my final presentation, I have to really think slightly more in a business perspective. The goal is to create a ‘new enterprise’. I have a bit more leeway with jargon as I will be presenting to designers but I think really thinking about what information would convince them and what would make sense for them to know could be a great guideline. At that point in the Summative, things like marketing and business strategy would have been established. I would use those as an anchor and starting point to expand on what message I’m trying to convey.

Week 5

01/06/26 - 07/06/26

What went well this sprint?

I have finally locked in a design direction for the app. The initial 2 moodboards I submitted had their own issues. The feedback was to keep my ‘cozy autumn’ colour palette but amend a primary colour. I got the idea to take and amend pink from the ‘pink lemonade’ palette and use that as a primary colour. I was really struggling with the direction of the palettes initially but I really clicked with this new direction and it was approved. I believe I mostly achieved my sprint goal barring the lo-fi user testing. However, I wanted to get the most out of my testing so I decided to delay and refine my lo-fi prototype first.

What did not go well this sprint?

I struggled with the direction of the colour palettes, and it may also be attributed to being unable to find ‘UI Inspirations’ with similar visual directions. I found this extra difficult due to the fact that we’re designing an app and not a website. I initially struggled with using websites as UI Inspirations just because they are so different from apps. I also got stuck for a very long time during this process because I just couldn’t progress without this. I also started looking at apps in general and their designs, but they seemed to look similar to me. Like there’s not as much variation or room to be different just because Apps are much more limited than Websites.

What did you learn?

I think in my head, there was such a disconnect between websites and apps for a currently unknown reason. In the last summative, I found individual UI element inspirations without relying on entire websites. I think I need to rethink that, it was so much easier to find UI inspirations once I started breaking down and looking for individual elements. E.g. a search bar or a way to display certain information or tags. Without realising it, I was overcomplicating my task and thought I needed an app that I can 90% visualise as an inspiration for the app I’m designing. Which then proved to be near impossible, and got me stuck.

What will you do next sprint, with this lesson in mind?

Depending on the outcome of my Lo-Fi user testing, I may need to amend, or include additional UI elements or configurations. I will now be looking for specific elements or configurations that will be used for iterations or my Hi-Fi prototype. I have also started my Lo-Fi with my style guide in mind, from my last summative’s reflection. This is so I don’t end up with too many font size and styles. Creating the moodboard, palettes and finding UI inspirations, have already put me in this mindset which I will be carrying through to my Hi-Fi prototype.
