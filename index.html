Weekly Retrospectives

Week 1

04/05/26 - 10/05/26

What went well this sprint?

I was able to locate a problem that is relatively niche and not overly used. The topic has the potential to diversify my design portfolio. As other topics explored seemed to favour minimalist design, which I have done plenty of prior. I was also able to identify and ask potential target users to interview. These are also people who are more easily accessible for follow ups and observations. I’ve accomplished my main goal of a Problem statement and research planning. There are some minor elements that will be caught up on, but I’m happy the main goals are reached.

What did not go well this sprint?

The area I have chosen was not my original choice. It was part of what I brainstormed. I think it took a little longer than I expected to lock in a problem and area. I struggled narrowing it down from my options. I think part of this is because I kept thinking forward and looking at why this might be difficult to do. E.g. lack of access to people I can interview, or the potential scope. I also struggled with information, there are not a lot of research available in this specific topic of ‘book slump’. Even with the journal articles, plenty of research are not aligned with what I’m looking for. E.g. research on readers with most articles looking at children or secondary school students. These will most likely not be part of target audience.

What did you learn?

I think being broader in choosing fields would be beneficial. I think I narrowed myself down by thinking forward on potentially why certain topics or problems may be difficult to tackle. It is probably still commendable to think of these issues initially. But it probably has to be more surface level as not to erase the option completely. Thinking about it now, once you start researching, or conducting user interviews, you might find a way to pivot and narrow down the problem further in a way this is more meaningful and manageable. This is the first sprint, so there currently is nothing to review, change or try currently.

What will you do next sprint, with this lesson in mind?

Again, being the first sprint there are not a lot of previous measures I can action. However, I will continue on what I mentioned previously about keeping it broad to not box myself in a certain direction. I will do this once I go further in to my secondary research as well as conducting user interviews or observations. I will have to ensure that my questions remain open and not lead my interviewees in a biased direction. I don’t believe I’m the most knowledgeable in this topic, and I will utilise this to keep my options open. This may be altering my problem statement or focusing on a specific aspect of it.
